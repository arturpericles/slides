What is the value in anonymity?

Anonymity as a second-best strategy for free speech

  • “a safety valve” (Froomkin) for “a flawed state of affairs” (Kreimer)

  • preventing speech from entering marketplace of ideas

  • anonymity as an absence

Anonymity and the weaponizing of the First Amendment

  • anonymity as a speaker right trumping listeners interests, to the detriment of democratic self-governance (Norton)

  • anonymity and online manipulation (Persily)

“A shield against the tyranny of the majority”: McIntyre

Earlier cases

  • Talley v California (1960) & prior restraint

  • NAACP v. Alabama ex rel. Patterson (1958)?

Majority opinion

  • “Anonymity is a shield against the tyranny of the majority. […] It thus exemplifies the purpose behind the Bill of Rights, and of the First Amendment in particular: to protect unpopular individuals from retaliation—and their ideas from suppression—at the hand of an intolerant society.”

  • “the interest in having anonymous works enter the marketplace of ideas unquestionably outweighs any public interest in requiring disclosure as a condition of entry”

  • England’s “abusive licensing laws”

anonymity as content

  • “an author’s decision to remain anonymous, like other decisions concerning omissions or additions to the content of a publication, is an aspect of the freedom of speech protected by the First Amendment.”

A general right to anonymity?

  • Scalia: limited exemption, predicated on a showing of legitimate fear of retaliation (e.g. NAACP v. Alabama ex rel. Patterson)

  • disclosure as viewpoint-neutral restriction

  • “How does the mask of anonymity claimed by someone who prefers to remain nameless or to publish under the disguise of a pseudonym advance that person’s self-development as an individual?” (Barendt)
